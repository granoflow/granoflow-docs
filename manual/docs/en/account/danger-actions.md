---
title: "Sign out, reset, and danger actions"
description: "Sign out, reset data, delete account — three actions with completely different consequences. Know the difference before you tap."
---

If you only want to switch devices or stop using this device for now, you usually only need to sign out. Do not treat sign out, reset local data, and delete account as the same action. They affect different data, and they differ in whether you can undo them.

These three actions are the easiest to confuse:

```text
Sign out ≠ Delete account ≠ Clear local data
```

## Sign out

Signing out means this device stops connecting to your account for now.

**What happens**: this device disconnects from your account, and cloud sync pauses.
**What does not happen**: tasks, journals, and projects on this device are not deleted just because you sign out.
**Can you undo it**: yes. Sign in to the same account again later.

If you are only switching devices, temporarily not using this device, or signing in again, sign out is usually enough.

## Reset local data

Resetting local data means clearing the data stored on this device.

**What happens**: local tasks, journals, settings, and other data on this device are cleared.
**What does not happen**: cloud data is not deleted just because you reset local data on this device. After signing in to the same account, you can sync cloud data again.
**Can you undo it**: no. Clearing local data is irreversible.

Use this only when you are sure you want to clear the local data on this device.

## Delete account

Deleting your account means closing the account and removing the association between the account and cloud data.

**What happens**: the account is closed, and cloud data and account associations are deleted; the deletion process has a grace period.
**What does not happen**: local copies on each device are not automatically cleared.
**Can you undo it**: after the grace period, recovery is not possible.

If you only want to switch devices, sign in again, or clean up one device, do not choose delete account.

## Recommended steps before acting

1. Confirm you have a recent backup (see “Backup and restore”).
2. If end-to-end encryption is enabled, confirm you have your encryption recovery key.
3. If you are only switching devices, sign out first; do not go straight to resetting local data or deleting the account.

:::caution[When in doubt, stop]
If you are unsure whether an action deletes your data, stop and read the relevant documentation, or contact support.
:::
