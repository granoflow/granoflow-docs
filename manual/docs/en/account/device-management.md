title: "Device management"
description: "View which devices are signed in to your account, and understand what removing a device actually does — and does not do."

If you want to see which devices are using your GranoFlow account, or stop one device from continuing to use this account, go to Device management to review and remove devices.

Device management lists the devices that are signed in to your GranoFlow account, such as your iPhone, iPad, or Mac. It helps you confirm which devices are currently connected to your account.

## What removing a device means

Removing a device means: **disconnecting your account from that device**.

After removal, that device will need to sign in again the next time it opens GranoFlow before it can continue using this account.

It does not mean:

- ❌ Local data on that device is deleted
- ❌ Tasks on that device are deleted
- ❌ Your GranoFlow account is canceled

## When to remove a device

You may want to remove a device in these situations:

- You switched phones and no longer use the old one
- You signed in to your account on a borrowed device
- Your device list shows a device you do not recognize
- You want to free up a device slot so you can sign in on a new device

## Checklist before removing

Before removing a device, check these two things:

- [ ] If you still need the content on that device, confirm it has synced to the cloud
- [ ] If that device will still use GranoFlow, sync will pause after removal until it signs in again

:::note[Device limits]
Some membership plans limit how many devices can be signed in at the same time. If you see a device limit prompt, remove an old device first, then sign in on the new one.
:::
