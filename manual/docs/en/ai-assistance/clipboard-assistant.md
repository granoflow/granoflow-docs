---
title: "AI assistant and clipboard"
description: "Hand copied text to AI and let it organize into tasks — preview and confirm before anything is written."
---

Use the clipboard assistant when you already copied a block of text and want to turn it into tasks: you open the assistant, GranoFlow reads the text currently held by the system clipboard, AI finds possible to-dos, and nothing is written to GranoFlow until you review, edit, and confirm the preview.

The clipboard itself is not a visible page inside GranoFlow. It is the system's temporary memory of what you copied most recently. That means the important step is not matching a screenshot; it is making sure you copied the right text and checking the preview before writing tasks.

## How to use it

1. Copy the content you want to organize, such as meeting notes, email snippets, chat messages, or quick notes.
2. Open GranoFlow's clipboard assistant.
3. AI analyzes the clipboard text and lists the parts it thinks look like to-dos.
4. Review the preview carefully. If anything is extra, missing, or misunderstood, adjust it first.
5. When everything looks right, confirm to write the tasks.

## What works well

- Action items from meeting notes
- Follow-ups from emails
- Commitments from chat conversations
- Scattered to-dos from personal notes

## Things to watch out for

- AI organizes tasks based on what it "understands" from the text, so it may misread background context as a to-do.
- Always check the preview before confirming. Do not rely only on the title or the number of items.
- Content is not sent in the background automatically; it enters the processing flow only when you actively trigger the clipboard assistant.
- Before writing, you can still delete, rewrite, or choose not to confirm the tasks.

:::tip[Do not want to process the whole block?]
Delete irrelevant parts from your clipboard first, keep only the content you actually want organized, then trigger the assistant.
:::
