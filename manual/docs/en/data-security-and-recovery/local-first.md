---
title: "What local-first means"
description: "GranoFlow stores data on your device first — it works without internet, and the cloud is a complement, not a requirement."
---

Local-first means GranoFlow stores your data on the current device first, so you can still open the app and view or edit local tasks, journals, and other content without an internet connection. Cloud sync helps move data between devices, but it is not required for the app to work.

Most cloud apps keep the main copy of your data on a server. Your phone or computer is mostly an entry point. Without internet, you may not see your data; if the server is temporarily unavailable, you may not be able to get in.

GranoFlow works the other way around: **data lives on your device first**. If the data is already on this device, you can keep using it when the network is offline.

## What local-first gives you

- **Use it without internet**: in the subway, on a plane, or in airplane mode, tasks and journals already stored on this device can still be viewed and edited
- **Faster opening**: the app does not need to wait for the network to load your data first, so local data usually appears faster
- **Less impact from temporary server issues**: even if GranoFlow's servers are temporarily unavailable, the local data on your device is still there

## What local-first cannot solve

- **Switching devices**: data on the old device does not automatically appear on a new device; you need cloud sync or a backup transfer
- **Device damage or loss**: if the data exists only on that device, losing the device may also mean losing the local data
- **Accidentally deleting a task**: if you delete something locally and it later syncs to the cloud, the cloud copy may also be deleted; recovery depends on an existing backup

So local-first does not mean “data can never be lost.” It solves “works without internet” and “data starts on your own device.” For switching devices, damaged devices, lost devices, or accidental deletion, you still need **cloud sync** and **manual backups**.

## Where local data is stored

Local data is stored in the app's private storage area. It is not stored in your photo library, downloads folder, or a normal file folder. This means:

- Uninstalling the app may delete local data on this device
- Whether system backups, such as iCloud or Google Backup, include app data depends on your system and backup settings

:::note[Recommended: export a backup]
Before upgrading your operating system, switching devices, or reinstalling the app, export a local backup from GranoFlow and save it somewhere you control.
:::
