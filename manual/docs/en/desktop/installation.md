---
title: "Desktop installation"
description: "Download and install the GranoFlow desktop app, and what to do if your system blocks it on first launch."
---

To use GranoFlow on your computer, download the installer from the GranoFlow website, install it, then sign in with the same account. The desktop app stays on your computer and can be opened with a shortcut, so you do not need to open a browser every time.

## Download and install

Open the [GranoFlow website](https://granoflow.com) and download the installer for your computer:

- **macOS**: download the `.dmg` file, open it, then drag GranoFlow into the Applications folder.
- **Windows**: download the `.exe` installer, then follow the setup wizard step by step.

## macOS says "cannot be opened" — what to do

The first time you open GranoFlow, macOS may say it is from an unidentified developer and block it from opening directly.

Do this:

1. Open Finder and find the GranoFlow app.
2. Right-click GranoFlow.
3. Choose "Open".
4. When macOS asks you to confirm, click "Open" again.

You can also handle it in System Settings: open "System Settings" → "Privacy & Security", find the blocked GranoFlow entry, then click "Open Anyway".

## Windows SmartScreen warning — what to do

The first time you run GranoFlow, Windows Defender SmartScreen may show a warning.

In that warning, click "More info", then click "Run anyway".

## After installation

After installation, open GranoFlow and sign in with your account. If this account already has cloud data, GranoFlow will start syncing that data.

:::note[Desktop and mobile share the same account]
When you sign in to the same account on desktop and mobile, your data is shared between devices through cloud sync.
:::
