If you only want the short answer: GranoFlow's core tasks, projects, journal, and local data features are usable for free; your data is stored on your device first; AI does not edit tasks automatically; cloud sync, some AI features, and multi-device use are subscription-related features.

## Basics

**What does GranoFlow do?**
GranoFlow helps you manage tasks, projects, journal entries, and reviews. It is not only for listing to-dos. It also helps you connect today's work with long-term goals, values, and project progress.

**How is GranoFlow different from a regular to-do app?**
A regular to-do app usually focuses on “what should I do” and “is it done.” GranoFlow also includes Journal and Review, so you can record what happened, reflect on it, and turn the next action back into a task.

**What does “Inbox” mean?**
Inbox is the default place for tasks that do not yet have a project or date. When you think of something, you can put it in the Inbox first and organize it later.

**What is the difference between a task and a Journal Entry?**
A Task is something you intend to complete, so it has a completion state. A Journal Entry is something you write down for reflection and review, so it does not have a “completed” state.

**Is there a task limit?**
Local tasks have no limit.

## Data and safety

**Where is my data stored?**
Your data is stored on your device first. If you enable cloud sync, it is also encrypted and synced to the cloud. GranoFlow uses end-to-end encryption, so the server cannot read your content.

**Is cloud sync the same as backup?**
No. Cloud sync follows your actions in real time: if you delete something, it is deleted in the cloud too. A backup is a point-in-time snapshot that you export manually, mainly for accidental deletion or keeping a copy before changing devices. They do not replace each other.

**If I switch phones, will my data still be there?**
You need cloud sync. Sign in to the new device with the same GranoFlow account, then enter the cloud sync key from the old device to access data synced to the cloud.

**What is my encryption key, and what if I forget it?**
The encryption key is the “key” that opens your encrypted cloud data. It is not your login password. Check your old device or password manager first. If the key is truly gone, your encrypted cloud data may be unrecoverable. You should view and save it now from the data/security area in Settings.

**Will signing out delete my data?**
No. Signing out only disconnects the account. Local data stays on the device, and you can sign in again later.

## AI assistance

**Will AI automatically edit my tasks?**
No. GranoFlow only writes AI suggestions into tasks or other fields after you explicitly confirm them. Suggestions you do not accept have no effect on your task data.

**Does GranoFlow send my data to AI?**
Only when you actively use an AI feature does the related text enter the AI processing flow, such as AI title parsing or the clipboard assistant. Browsing tasks, writing journal entries, and doing reviews do not involve AI.

**Do AI features require a subscription?**
Most AI features require a subscription, including title parsing, clipboard assistant, and redaction terms.

**What are Redaction Terms?**
Redaction Terms are a replacement table like “sensitive term → code name.” Before text is sent to an external AI, GranoFlow replaces sensitive terms; when the result comes back, it restores them. This is a subscription feature.

## Subscription

**Can I use GranoFlow without subscribing?**
Yes. Core features such as tasks, projects, journal, and local storage are free to use. Subscription mainly unlocks cloud sync, multi-device use, and some AI assistance features.

**Will my data disappear if my subscription expires?**
No. Local data stays on your device. Some subscription features may pause, but your local data is not deleted because a subscription expires.

**I bought a subscription, but the benefits did not appear. What should I do?**
First check that the GranoFlow account you are signed in to now is the same account used for the purchase. Benefits are tied to the account, not to a specific device.

**If I subscribe on iOS, can I use it on Android?**
Not directly. iOS, Android, and macOS are separate payment platforms, and purchases are independent. After signing in to the same GranoFlow account, you can share the matching benefits across multiple devices on the same platform.

**How do I restore a purchase?**
Go to the subscription page in Settings and choose Restore purchases. Before restoring, make sure the signed-in account is the same account used for the purchase.

## Desktop

**Does closing the window quit GranoFlow?**
No. Closing the window only hides the interface. GranoFlow may keep running and syncing in the background. To fully quit, right-click the tray or Dock icon and choose Quit.

**Is desktop data the same as mobile data?**
If you sign in with the same account and enable cloud sync, desktop and mobile data stay aligned through cloud sync.

## Other

**Is GranoFlow suitable for GTD?**
Yes. GranoFlow supports core GTD ideas such as Inbox capture, project organization, and next actions. It does not force you to follow one specific method.

**Is GranoFlow suitable for habit building?**
Yes. You can write micro-habits as low-friction tasks, connect them to projects and values, and use daily review for feedback. It is not a habit-tracking calendar; it is better for connecting habits to a long-term goal system.
