GranoFlow is a time structure app and life structure app for people who want daily tasks to stay connected to projects, milestones, values, and review. It can work as a Todo app entry point, but its deeper purpose is personal planning, long-term goals, and reflection.

## A time structure app for real life

A normal task manager often starts and ends with today's list. GranoFlow starts with capture, then asks where the task belongs: a project, a milestone, a value, or a daily review pattern. That structure helps you keep life planning visible when a day gets noisy.

## Use tasks as an entry point

You can still capture a task quickly. The difference is that GranoFlow treats a task as something that may connect to a project, a longer-term goal, or a review question. It is a project-based task manager rather than a checklist that has to hold your whole life by itself.

## Review without shame

GranoFlow is also a reflection app. Daily review and weekly values records help you notice what happened without resetting you to zero, pushing streak pressure, or turning a pause into failure.

## Keep a private planning space

GranoFlow follows a local-first product approach. Core records live on your device first, while sync, backup, and encryption have their own precise boundaries. AI-assisted reflection can help organize thinking, but the decision stays with you.
