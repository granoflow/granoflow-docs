If you want to know how GranoFlow works, this manual will guide you from the most basic recording: first write down what you need to do today, then place it into projects, domains, milestones, and reviews, gradually forming your life system. GranoFlow is not just a tool for listing Todos; it also integrates light journaling to help you see your rhythm, sources of stress, and what you truly value. It already has built-in AI task parsing and thought sorting features, and more AI-related uses will be rolled out later.

## Start with a Life System

When you first start, read "Start in 5 Minutes". You will learn three things: how to capture a task, how to decide which project or life domain it belongs to, and how to end the day with a light review. Record today first; you don't need to build a complete system from the beginning. As records accumulate, it will become easier for you to see your values, habits, and long-term direction.

## Use the Manual for Real Decisions

This manual is suitable to consult when you need to make decisions. For example: how to set up domains and values, how to break goals into projects, milestones, and tasks, how daily reviews and light journals help you discover behavior patterns, and how to cultivate habits and enter flow without increasing anxiety. The manual also explains the differences between local-first and cloud-first productivity tools, encryption boundaries, and the limits of AI-assisted reviews.

If you are already using reviews to accumulate experience, you can also read [Cards: Bringing Experience Back to Action](/manual/en/review-cards/). The cards chapter explains how to organize experiences from tasks and reviews into reusable knowledge cards, and then bring those experiences back into future actions through practice, related tasks, and card deck management.

## Face Life with ACT

GranoFlow does not promise to automatically turn you into the person you want to be. What it offers is a structure: first accept reality, then see clearly what you truly value, then commit to the next action, and place that action into projects, milestones, tasks, and reviews. The person you want to become is not a one-time setup result, but a direction that gradually grows through recording, choosing, acting, and reviewing.
