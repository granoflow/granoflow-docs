If you want to adjust how GranoFlow feels on this device, look mainly at "Preferences" and "Security" on the Settings page. The switches here only affect the current device; they won't change your tasks, projects, or review data, nor will they automatically sync to other devices.

When you switch to a new phone, tablet, or computer, these preferences won't follow. You'll need to check them again on the new device.

## Explanation of each setting

### Timer background sound

The timer background sound in "Preferences" controls whether this device plays background sounds during focus timer sessions.

It only affects your experience when using the timer; it does not change recorded time investment data.

### App lock

When you enable App lock in "Security", each time you open GranoFlow, you must authenticate with this device's local verification, such as Face ID, fingerprint, or PIN.

It's suitable for shared devices or when you briefly step away from your device, reducing the chance of others seeing content directly.

Note: App lock is not an account password, nor does it replace encryption keys or system-level security settings.

### Messages & reminders

Under "Preferences", "Messages & reminders" centrally controls task reminders and Notification Center messages on this device.

Task reminders let you control whether to show system banners and whether to play a sound when a banner appears. Turning off banners does not delete tasks or clear already-set reminder times on tasks; the sound switch only works when banners are enabled.

Notification Center messages only go to the in-app notification list by default; they do not pop up as system banners or play sounds. Here you can separately enable system banners for Notification Center messages; the sound switch only takes effect after banners are enabled.

Silent sync is used to backfill new data from other devices in the background, but it's a default compensation capability of cloud sync; it is not toggled on or off under "Messages & reminders". It stays silent—no banners, no sounds, and no new entries in Notification Center.

### Reduce bottom prompts

Under "Preferences", "Reduce bottom prompts" controls some bottom feedback after you've performed familiar actions, such as the brief "Operation successful" prompt after moving a task from the inbox to another location.

Turning it off does not cancel the move operation, nor does it hide error prompts. It just removes that small green "Done" notification.

### Other local entries

Under "Preferences" you might also see entries for AI assistant, command-line tool, custom tag management, card internalization operation confirmation, etc. They lead to their respective setting or management pages; do not interpret these entries as the same sync switch. When you need to handle remote rich-text resource prompts, go to "Pro Settings", where there is an independent switch controlling whether this device asks again before loading remote resources.
