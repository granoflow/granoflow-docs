The "Progress" page lets you know as soon as you open GranoFlow: what needs attention now, what progress you've made today, whether you've been consistent this week and this month, and the current state of your ongoing projects.

It's not a task list or a statistics report. Think of it as a home dashboard: take a quick glance, then decide whether to dive into today's tasks or review yesterday.

## Home status & today's review

At the top of the page, you'll first see your work queue and card study entry to help you decide what to do next. Below that is the "Progress snapshot", which shows yesterday's and today's completion counts, project count, and time invested; if you're already using card study, the snapshot also supplements card study progress, distribution of not studied / studying / mastered / internalized, today's due reviews, and recently reviewed counts. Below the snapshot is a lightweight "Today's review" card: when there's a diagnosis, it shows a line about today's investment stage, up to two behavior tags, and a button to enter today's review; on weekends or at month end, entries for weekly or monthly review also appear in this same card when they are available. If there's no diagnosis but any daily, weekly, or monthly review can be recorded or has already been completed, the card still keeps the write, view, or completed state. On desktop landscape, these current status items are aligned on the left, while the right continues to display longer‑term information such as projects, directions & domains, this week, and this month.

The "Today's review" card is only for quick judgment; it doesn't expand into a full analysis. For a more complete reason, record, or reflection, use the daily, weekly, monthly, or review calendar entry inside the card to go to the review page.

## Card study

The card study entry appears only once at the top of the Progress page. It shows the total active cards and today's due review count; if there are cards due, you can start studying; if there are no cards or no due content, it guides you to card management or status view. Tap the total card count to enter "Card statistics" and see study status, future review workload, and recent review activity. The card study progress and card numbers in the progress snapshot are only status supplements, not a second review entry.

Card study statuses are: not studied, studying, mastered, internalized. Internalized means a mastered card has been brought back to real projects: tasks from 3 different projects are associated with the same card. It reminds you that this insight has not only been remembered but also used across different projects and can return as experience for future actions.

In "Card statistics", you can continue to card review or open "Card management". If you want to understand why cards are grouped with tasks, reviews, and projects, read [Cards: bring experience back to action](/manual/review-cards/); if you want to see the difference between study, mastery, and internalization, read [Study, mastery & internalization](/manual/review-cards/study-and-internalize/).

## This week & this month status

The Progress page also shows this week's and this month's progress. Its focus is not to present a stressful ranking or score, but to help you see whether you've been consistent recently.

If a certain domain hasn't appeared for several consecutive days, you can reflect during your review: is this normal scheduling, or has it been overlooked?

## Project progress cards

If you are working on projects, the Progress page shows related project cards. This way you can get a rough idea of which projects have recent activity without opening each project page individually.

## What it's good for

The Progress page is best at answering this question:

> Have I been steadily advancing the things I care about?

If you just want to quickly check status, the Progress page is enough. If you want to dig into causes and adjust your schedule, go to the review page.

:::note[Progress does not replace review]
The Progress page is a quick glance entry; the Review page is where you think deeply. Using them together works best.
:::
