Granoflow's main interface follows one workflow: capture → organize → move forward → review.

When the app starts, it opens the Progress page by default. Here you can see your current status, recent progress, and items that still need attention.

## Bottom Navigation (Left Side in Landscape)

The bottom navigation has four main entries:

<!-- manual-screenshot:id=interface-root-route -->
![Interface overview screen](../../../screenshots/en/interface-root-route.png)

- Progress: View your overall current status and recent activity.
- Tasks: View and move through your task list.
- Review: Open daily review, weekly review, and the monthly calendar view.
- Settings: Manage your account, sync, data, AI assistance, appearance, and other preferences.

The `+` in the middle of the bottom bar is the quick add entry point. Tap it to write down one thing, then optionally set a date, project, milestone, and tags. It does not switch you to a separate page; it helps you quickly capture a task from your current screen.

## Top-Left Menu

The top-left menu opens more entries:

- Inbox: Temporarily stores ideas and tasks you just captured, so you can write them down first and organize them later.
- Task list: View the tasks you are actively moving forward in one place.
- Completed: View items you have already completed.
- Archived: View archived tasks or project content.
- Trash: View deleted items that are waiting for handling.
- Domain management: Manage long-term life domains and values you care about.
- Project management: Manage projects, milestones, and stage goals.

## Layout on Different Screens

On wide screens or in desktop mode, the interface may appear as side navigation with a multi-column layout. On narrow screens or phones, you mainly work through the bottom navigation, the top-left menu, and the bottom `+` button. Visual details may differ across platforms, but the relationship between entry points stays the same.

## First-Time Use

If this is your first time using the app, start by tapping the `+` in the middle of the bottom bar and writing down the clearest thing in front of you. After that, decide whether it is just a regular task or whether it belongs in a project, milestone, or long-term domain.
