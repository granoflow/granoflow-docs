Account, sync, data, subscription, and AI-related entries in Settings usually open more specific pages. This page first explains what each entry does in one sentence, then points you to the fuller rules in the relevant sections.

If an action involves restore, delete, sync reset, keys, subscription entitlements, or signing out, read the relevant page before continuing.

## Account Entry

The account entry is for signing up, signing in, signing out, checking account status, and connecting the current device to the same account system.

After signing in, you can purchase or restore GranoFlow Pro entitlements, enable account-required cloud sync capabilities, and access account-related personalization. Read [Account overview](/manual/en/account/overview/) to see what an account can do; read [Device management](/manual/en/account/device-management/) to understand the relationship between the current device and other devices.

## Sync Entry

The sync entry keeps core records such as tasks, projects, and reviews consistent across devices.

Sync does not simply “copy every local setting elsewhere.” It mainly handles the movement of business records. Current-device preferences such as language, theme, font, and app lock are in a different scope. Read [Multi-device sync](/manual/en/data-security-and-recovery/sync/) to see what syncs and what to check first when sync behaves unexpectedly.

## Data and Recovery Entry

The data entry is for import, export, backup, restore, checking attachment status, or clearing local storage use.

These actions are usually more sensitive than appearance and device preferences. Backup helps preserve important data when changing devices, reinstalling, or recovering from an issue; restore brings backup or cloud data back to the current device. Before restoring, confirm the backup source, account status, key, and version conditions. Read [Backup and restore](/manual/en/data-security-and-recovery/backup-and-restore/) for details.

## Subscription Entry

The subscription entry is for checking GranoFlow Pro entitlements, purchase status, restore purchase guidance, and limits that may come from different platforms.

Pro entitlements may affect cloud sync, attachment capabilities, storage quota, or access to advanced configuration. Actual prices and availability follow the platform UI. Read [Subscription overview](/manual/en/subscription/overview/) to understand why subscriptions exist, and [Subscription entitlements](/manual/en/subscription/entitlements/) to see entitlement boundaries.

## AI Assistant and Tag Management

The AI assistant entry is for choosing or configuring the external AI tool you want to use with GranoFlow, such as handing prepared content to ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, DeepSeek, or a custom assistant. It does not mean AI will automatically read all local data, and it does not mean AI will silently change your records. Read [AI assistance](/manual/en/ai-assistance/overview/) for the overall boundaries, and [AI assistant and clipboard](/manual/en/ai-assistance/clipboard-assistant/) for the clipboard flow.

Tag management is for creating, renaming, organizing, or disabling task tags, so you can organize tasks across contexts, places, energy levels, or themes. Tags affect task organization, so they should not be treated as appearance settings. Read [Tags](/manual/en/tasks/tags/) to see how tags help organize tasks.
