Many people do not fail from ignorance.
They fail after interruption and then cannot return.

This page solves one thing:

> After interruption, how to continue without collapse-repayment.

## Stable engagement is not “never stop”

Stable engagement means:

- You can still find the entry after stopping.
- You know where to resume tomorrow.

## Why small actions work

Big goals are often too heavy and too vague to start.

Shrink actions to lower start friction:

- Write 100 words
- Walk 10 minutes
- Clean one task

Small action is not lowering ambition. It lowers start cost.

## How projects/milestones prevent scattering

- Project answers what this period is for.
- Milestone answers which stage is first.

So you face today’s stage-step, not your whole life goal.

## Minimal return method after interruption

Do not repay backlog first. Do three steps:

1. One reality line: where I stopped
2. One smallest action: what I do now
3. One review line: what next after this

You need direction recovery, not perfect catch-up.

## 30-Second Flow Readiness Check

Before starting, check:

- Is the goal clear?
- Is challenge appropriate?
- Is feedback visible?
- Is return path clear after interruption?

If 2+ are "no", shrink task or add feedback marker first.

## Short example

- Value: care for body over time
- Project: 3-month exercise rhythm
- Milestone: week-one adaptation
- Today task: 5-minute stretch
- Review: low friction, can continue tomorrow

This is the minimal stable-engagement loop.
