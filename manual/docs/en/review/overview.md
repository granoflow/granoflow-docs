If you just want a quick review, open the review page, look at what you completed, and write a few notes; daily, weekly, and monthly review are simply different time ranges, and you do not need to fill everything in every day.

For many people, “retrospective” sounds stressful, as if it needs to be complete and insightful. GranoFlow review is not like that. Think of it as a very light journal: glance at what is already done, note how it felt or what comes next, then stop.

## What is inside a review

- **What you completed today**: tracked by actual completion time, not due date
- **Your notes**: feelings, thoughts, and next steps in your own words
- **Values prompts**: a way to connect today’s actions back to your long-term direction

## How to do a review

1. Open the review page.
2. Choose daily, weekly, or monthly review; for a daily review, you can choose a date, and it defaults to today. For a weekly review, the detail page header shows the week number and date range. For a monthly review, switch to the month you want, then open the monthly overview. The detail page header shows that year and month.
3. Glance at the completed tasks list.
4. Write a few sentences. For example: “Tough day, but I finished the most important thing.”
5. Read the values prompts and think about whether today’s actions moved toward what matters to you.
6. Close the page. The review is done.

You do not have to do every step. It is fine to only check the list, avoid writing a long entry, or skip a prompt.

## What review is not

- Not a check-in system: missing days does not invalidate your tasks
- Not a performance report: no one is grading you
- Not a task list: it helps you digest what is already done, not add more tasks

:::tip[The shortest review still has value]
Even “Finished X today, will start Y tomorrow” accomplishes the core purpose of review.
:::
