Ever notice your review answers getting more and more perfunctory? Same questions, same answers, day after day?

These settings let your daily review, weekly review, journal, values prompts, and questionnaires speak your language — not the system default.

![Values and prompts settings](../../../screenshots/en/review-values-prompts-settings.png)

## What prompt settings do

Think of them as "instructions to your AI assistant." When you use AI features to organize your journal, generate a weekly summary, or extract action insights, the AI follows these prompts to understand your preferences.

Each scenario has its own prompt:

| Scenario | What it shapes |
|----------|---------------|
| Daily review rewrite | Requirements when organizing the day's notes |
| Weekly review | How a week of records gets summarized |
| Domain values | How AI explores your long-term values |
| Work and learning report | Structure of the report draft |

After you edit a prompt, later uses of that feature read the new text. Existing tasks, records, and historical summaries are not automatically rewritten.

## Questionnaire and values settings

Analysis and questionnaire settings control things like when a review questionnaire is finalized — helping you close the day into a more stable record without judging whether the day was good or bad.

Domain values settings bring your long-term direction into review context. Values can be edited at any time and become clearer as real records accumulate. They are not a classification table that must be completed once and remain correct forever.

## Limits to keep in mind

- **Prompts do not guarantee AI quality**: Changing a prompt guides direction but does not guarantee accuracy.
- **No effect on tasks or projects**: These settings only shape future prompts, drafts, and questions — not existing records.
- **Some settings are members-only**: Non-members can view the defaults but cannot customize them.

:::tip[Not sure where to start?]
Begin with the journal prompt — tell the AI your preferred writing style or note-taking habits, and you will notice the difference immediately.
:::
