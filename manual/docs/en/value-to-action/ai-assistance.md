This page supports the values-to-action path. GranoFlow can connect long-term goals, tasks, projects, and review; account, sync, AI, and membership features only make that personal planning practice clearer and more controllable. They do not decide your values or change data without your confirmation.

GranoFlow does not treat AI as someone who lives your life for you.

AI (artificial intelligence) is better at organizing, summarizing, asking questions, and drafting. It can help you see patterns in your records, but it should not decide your life direction for you or directly change data without your confirmation.

GranoFlow's approach to AI assistance is simple:

> Your records stay in your hands. AI only helps organize them.

## What AI Is Good For

AI is good at working with content you have already written down.

For example:

- summarize what you completed today
- organize a week of project progress
- find repeatedly delayed tasks from reviews
- turn scattered records into a daily or weekly report
- help clarify values within a domain
- suggest candidate next actions based on tasks and projects

These jobs are not about letting AI decide what matters most. They help make your material clearer.

You still need to decide:

- which summaries are accurate
- which suggestions are useful
- which content should not be kept
- what the next step should actually be

AI can help you see patterns, but it cannot take responsibility for your choices.

## What AI Is Not Good For

AI is not good at directly making life judgments for you.

For example:

- deciding whether you should drop a project
- judging whether a relationship is worth continuing
- deciding your career direction
- reordering all tasks and executing the plan directly
- writing values you do not actually agree with

AI can ask questions, organize information, and offer angles for these topics, but the final confirmation should still be yours.

GranoFlow's goal is not to let AI manage you. It is to help you manage your own time structure more clearly.

## Why Copy and Confirmation Matter

GranoFlow prefers AI assistance to stay visible and controllable.

It does not secretly write external AI responses into your tasks, projects, or reviews.

A safer flow is:

1. GranoFlow prepares the prompt and necessary material
2. You copy or send it to an external AI
3. AI generates a summary, draft, or suggestion
4. You copy the result back
5. GranoFlow recognizes the content
6. You confirm before importing or using it

This adds one confirmation step, but the benefit is clear:

> Data is not changed by AI before you have seen it.

For personal records, values, reviews, and long-term projects, that matters.

## Use AI for Daily Review

Daily review is a good place to let AI turn your notes into clearer writing.

For example, if you completed several tasks today and wrote some scattered notes, you can ask AI to summarize:

- what you mainly completed today
- which project moved forward
- which tasks were not completed
- what step could come first tomorrow
- what clues appeared about your mood and state

You can use a prompt like this:

```text
Please use the following GranoFlow records to organize a short daily review.

Requirements:
1. Do not exaggerate or invent anything.
2. Summarize only from the content I provide.
3. Use three sections: "Completed Today", "Worth Noting", and "Next Step".
4. If there is not enough information, say so directly.

Records:
...
```

After AI generates a draft, keep only the useful parts.

Do not treat AI output as fact. It is only a draft organized from the material you provided.

## Use AI for Weekly Review

Weekly review is better for seeing trends.

You can ask AI to help answer:

- which projects mainly moved forward this week
- which domains received the most attention
- which tasks were delayed repeatedly
- which plans are no longer important
- what is most worth prioritizing next week

The prompt can look like this:

```text
Please use the following GranoFlow weekly records to organize a weekly review.

Focus on:
1. Projects that truly moved forward this week.
2. Items that appeared repeatedly but did not move forward.
3. Actions that moved closer to my values.
4. The 1-3 things most worth prioritizing next week.

Requirements:
- Do not make the final decision for me.
- Keep suggestions specific and actionable.
- Do not invent information that is not in the records.

Records:
...
```

The point of a weekly review is not to make the writing look good. It is to help you see where the week actually went.

## Use AI to Clarify Values

Many people cannot write values at the beginning.

That is normal.

You can let AI act as a questioner and help you see what you care about through your experiences.

For example:

```text
I am using GranoFlow to organize my domains and values.

Please do not directly write a life manifesto for me.
Based on my answers, ask questions step by step to help me see:
1. What I am willing to invest in over the long term.
2. What makes me feel more like myself after completing it.
3. What is difficult but still something I do not want to give up.
4. What value statements could look like in each domain.

Ask only 1-2 questions at a time, then wait for my answer before continuing.
```

This is better than asking AI to generate "the top ten values" directly.

Values are not beautiful sentences. They are directions you are willing to keep moving toward.

## Use AI to Generate Reports

If you need to explain progress to someone else, AI can help turn GranoFlow records into a more formal report.

For example:

- daily report
- weekly report
- monthly report
- project progress report
- learning summary
- stage retrospective

You can ask AI to use a fixed format:

```text
Please turn the following GranoFlow records into a work weekly report.

Format:
1. Completed this week
2. Project progress
3. Problems encountered
4. Plan for next week
5. Help needed

Requirements:
- Keep it objective.
- Do not add anything that is not in the records.
- Do not write it like exaggerated marketing copy.
- If a section has no material, write "No clear record yet".

Records:
...
```

These reports are useful for communication, but do not let them take over how you record.

GranoFlow's core is still personal structure, not being evaluated by someone else.

## Privacy and Boundaries

Before sending content to an external AI, first decide whether that content is suitable to send out.

Pay special attention to:

- private journals
- other people's privacy
- company confidential information
- unpublished business plans
- identity information
- sensitive health or emotional records

If you are not sure, do not send the full content.

You can remove sensitive parts first, or provide only a summary.

For example, do not directly send:

> A person's real name, email, company internal information, and full chat logs

Instead, write:

> A collaborator raised several issues, and I want to organize my response.

AI assistance should be based on you clearly knowing what you are sending.

## Do Not Let AI Replace Review

AI can help organize review, but it cannot complete review for you.

Real review happens when you judge questions like:

- Is this important to me?
- Am I still willing to keep investing in it?
- Which actions are closer to my values?
- Which busyness is actually avoidance?
- What next step am I willing to commit to?

AI can offer angles, but it does not know who you truly want to become.

So the best question is not:

> What do you think I should do?

It is:

> Based on my records, please list a few possible explanations and next-step options. I will decide.

In this flow, AI is the organizer, not the judge.
